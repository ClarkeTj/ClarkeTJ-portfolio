About

I'm Clarke-Efayena Tejiri (22), a 400L Computer Science student at FUPRE — a developer who treats systems like positions on a chessboard: evaluate, calculate, execute. I build clean, smooth and responsive interfaces with solid front-end foundations.

My coding philosophy is simple — small, composable pieces, accessible design, and data-driven decisions. Chess has shaped my pattern recognition, time management, and patience — skills that flow naturally into coding.

My love for both Chess and Coding drove me to build this platform for my school’s chess club. While studying several coding courses, I often heard tutors tell beginners to build from what they’re passionate about — because passion makes projects stand out with originality and purpose, not just as clones or random apps. That mindset helped shape this project into something truly personal.

Joining YellowDot gave me hands-on experience working with developers and teammates, which fueled my growth and inspired me to bring this vision to life — a platform where our club can track games, h2h records (which comes along with some bantering and bragging rights), leaderboards, tournaments, and event highlights. What started as a simple homepage gradually evolved into a full project with interactive pages and features.

Technologies I work with include HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and React — with growing experience in responsive design and version control (Git/GitHub).

My next goal is to deepen my skills in React and backend integration, so I can turn more creative ideas into fully functional platforms.

I plan to keep learning, studying, and improving my skillsets, in both coding and chess. Crossing the 2k Elo mark was a milestone — now I aim even higher. I’m grateful to God, my family, and friends for their support and every smile along the way.

Whether on the board or behind the screens, I’m always looking for the next best move.
